This centuries-old practice originated in colonial times when marriages required formal family agreements and continues today as the first celebration in the wedding journey.

The modern La Pedida process includes:

Andean Wedding Rituals

Despachodes-PAH-choh Ceremony to Pachamamapah-chah-MAH-mah

The despacho ceremony is a sacred Andean offering ritual to Pachamama (Mother Earth) that involves burning or burying symbolic items while making wishes for the couple’s prosperity, typically lasting 1-3 hours and costing $200-$500 USD. This pre-Columbian tradition reflects the Andean principle of ayniAHY-neequ-PE_ayni or reciprocity with nature.

Despacho ceremony process:

  1. Spread ceremonial cloth: Usually a traditional textile (10-15 minutes)
  2. Place symbolic offerings: CocaKOH-kah leaves, flowers, seeds, candies (30-45 minutes)
  3. Guest participation: Each person adds coca leaves with blessings (20-30 minutes)
  4. Shaman’s prayers: In Quechua and Spanish (15-20 minutes)
  5. Burning or burial: Final offering to Pachamama (10-15 minutes)

Essential despacho elements and costs:

  • Coca leaves (1-2 pounds): $20-$40 USD
  • Flowers (specific colors): $30-$50 USD
  • Food offerings: $50-$100 USD
  • Shaman fee: $100-$300 USD
