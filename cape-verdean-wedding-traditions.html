Cape Verde Wedding Traditions

These Creole cultural celebrations encompass pre-wedding batukubah-TOO-koo dance gatherings, emotional morna de despedidaMOR-nah dee desh-peh-DEE-dah farewell ceremonies, civil or Catholic religious ceremonies, and energetic funanáfoo-nah-NAH reception parties, with total costs ranging from 500,000-2,000,000 CVE ($4,900-$19,600 USD).

Conta d’odjo is a post-wedding protection ritual lasting 1 hour with close family members, involving minimal cost for bead purchases, where odd-numbered beads are blessed and given to newlyweds for wearing as protection against misfortune.

The “eye beads” tradition derives from West African beliefs adapted through Portuguese Catholic influence. Beads must total odd numbers (typically 3, 5, or 7) and feature specific color combinations: blue for protection, red for passion, and white for purity. Creole families strongly maintain this practice, with 80% of couples receiving conta d’odjo regardless of religious affiliation.

Modern interpretations transform protective beads into fashion accessories, with jewelry designers creating elaborate versions costing 20,000-50,000 CVE ($196-$490 USD).
