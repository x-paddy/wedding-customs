Symbolic Wedding Traditions

Las Arraslahs AH-rahs: The Thirteen Coins Tradition

Las Arras is the Venezuelan wedding tradition of exchanging 13 gold or chocolate coins during the ceremony, symbolizing Jesus and the twelve apostles while representing the couple’s commitment to mutual financial support. This Spanish colonial tradition, practiced in 75% of Venezuelan religious ceremonies, involves the groom presenting coins to the bride in an ornate box or pouch.

Las Arras specifications:

  • Number of coins: Always 13
  • Material: Gold (traditional) or chocolate (modern)
  • Container: Silver or gold arras box ($50-$300 USD)
  • Symbolism: Prosperity, faith, mutual support
  • Regional variations: Coastal regions prefer gold, inland areas use chocolate

El Lazoel LAH-soh: The Unity Lasso

El Lazo is a large rosary or decorative rope placed over the couple’s shoulders in a figure-eight pattern during Venezuelan Catholic weddings, symbolizing their eternal bond. This tradition, observed in 65% of religious ceremonies, represents the infinite nature of marriage through its infinity symbol shape.

El Lazo details:

  • Material: Pearl rosary, silk rope, or flower garland
  • Placement: After vows, before rings
  • Duration: 5-10 minutes of ceremony
  • Cost: $50-$500 USD depending on materials
  • Keepsake: Often displayed in couple’s home
