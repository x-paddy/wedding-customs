The Church-to-Beach Pipeline: How One Day Holds Two Different Worlds

How much does a typical wedding in Antigua and Barbuda cost?

Great question-and the answer varies wildly! Local weddings typically run EC$13,500-$81,000 ($5,000-$30,000 USD), depending heavily on guest count and venue dreams. What documents do I need to get married in Antigua and Barbuda?

The paperwork is refreshingly straightforward, but you absolutely must apply in person at the Ministry of Justice in St. John’s-no online applications accepted. Required documents include valid passports for both of you (no exceptions, even for residents). If you’re divorced, bring that original Decree Absolute-photocopies won’t work, and yes, they check. Widowed? You’ll need both the original marriage certificate and death certificate.

The beautiful part? No blood tests, no waiting periods, no residency requirements. Some nationalities might need a single status affidavit from their embassy, but that’s usually sorted in a day. You can literally fly in Monday, apply Tuesday morning, and be married by Tuesday afternoon. One couple we know landed at 2 PM, were at the Ministry by 3 PM, and married on the beach at sunset the next day. That’s island efficiency at its finest!

Can we have just a religious ceremony without the civil ceremony?

No, and this is non-negotiable-the civil ceremony must happen first, no matter how beautiful your beach pastor or how historic your chosen church. Antigua and Barbuda law requires that government ceremony between 6am-6pm with two witnesses aged 18+. It costs EC$950 ($350 USD), takes about 15-30 minutes, and yes, those fluorescent lights are exactly as unromantic as they sound.

But here’s the thing: locals see it as doubling the celebration rather than doubling the hassle. Most couples do their civil ceremony one day, then have their religious or beach celebration the next day or even later that week. The religious ceremony typically costs an additional EC$1,350-$5,400 ($500-$2,000 USD) including church fees, choir, and decorations. Think of the civil ceremony as the legal appetizer before your spiritual main course!

When is the best time of year for a wedding in Antigua?

December through May offers postcard-perfect weather with minimal rain and temperatures hovering between 75-85¬∞F-pure paradise. This is high season, though, meaning prices jump 20-40% and popular venues book solid. The sweet spot? Late April through May, when you still get gorgeous weather but rates start dropping as hotels prepare for summer.

Hurricane season officially runs June through November, with September and October being the riskiest months. But here’s what they don’t tell you: early June often has beautiful weather at significantly lower prices, and November can be stunning once storm season wraps up. We’ve seen couples save thousands by choosing June 1st over May 31st! If you’re flexible, ask venues about their hurricane policies-many offer free rebooking if storms threaten.

What is Black Cake and why does it take months to prepare?

Imagine the richest, most decadent fruit cake you’ve ever tasted, then soak it in premium rum for half a year-that’s Black Cake (blak-kaykBLAK-kayk), and it’s not optional at an Antiguan wedding! This Caribbean interpretation of British Christmas pudding requires soaking dried fruits (prunes, raisins, currants, cherries) in rum and cherry brandy for 3-6 months minimum. No shortcuts-the fruit needs time to fully absorb the alcohol and transform into something magical.

The actual baking happens about a week before the wedding, using the rum-drunk fruits ground into paste, mixed with molasses and burnt sugar (that’s where the “black” comes from), and baked low and slow for 4-6 hours. The result? A dense, moist cake that’s basically adult candy, traditionally covered in white fondant to look innocent. Each slice packs a punch-we’re talking 40-proof cake here! Expect to pay $150-$500 USD depending on size, and trust us, guests will be talking about it years later.
