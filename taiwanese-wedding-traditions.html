Taiwan Wedding Traditions

What Are Taiwanese Wedding Traditions?

Taiwanese wedding traditions are multi-day celebrations that blend Chinese Confucian customs with local Taiwanese practices, typically lasting 2-7 days and involving 100-500 guests from both families. These celebrations encompass pre-wedding rituals like the betrothal gift exchange (guo da ligwoh-dah-lee), the formal tea ceremony (jing chajing-chah), wedding day festivities including door games (chuangmenchwahng-men), and elaborate banquet receptions (xi yanshee-yen) featuring 8-12 symbolic dishes.

Overview of Taiwanese Wedding Process

  • 12 months before: Consult fortune teller for auspicious date selection
  • 6 months before: Formal proposal (nazainah-dzai) and family negotiations begin
  • 3 months before: Betrothal gift exchange (guo da li) ceremony
  • 1 month before: Pre-wedding photography sessions
  • 1 day before: Hair combing ceremony (shu toushoo-toh)
  • Wedding day: Tea ceremony, civil registration, banquet reception
  • 3 days after: Bride’s return visit to parents (san chaosahn-chow)

Pre-Wedding Traditions and Ceremonies

Finding an Auspicious Date: The Foundation of Wedding Planning

Auspicious date selection is a crucial wedding planning tradition that involves consulting lunar calendars and fortune tellers, typically occurring 6-12 months before the wedding and costing 2,000-5,000 TWD ($65-160 USD).

Cost and Consultation Process

  • Traditional consultation: 3,000-5,000 TWD ($100-160 USD) for fortune teller
  • Modern alternatives: 500-2,000 TWD ($15-65 USD) for online calculators
  • Factors considered: Couple’s birth dates, zodiac signs, lunar calendar
  • Timeline: 2-3 consultation sessions over 1 month

The Date Selection Process

  1. Initial consultation: Fortune teller analyzes couple’s ba zibah-dzuheight characters based on birth time
  2. Calendar review: Identification of 3-5 potential dates avoiding conflict days
  3. Family approval: Both families confirm date suits their schedules
  4. Venue booking: Selected date guides all subsequent planning

Regional Variations

Northern Taiwan practice emphasizes strict adherence to lunar calendar calculations, with 85% of traditional families consulting professional fortune tellers for date selection.

Southern Taiwan approach often incorporates local temple consultations alongside fortune telling, adding spiritual blessings to the date selection process.

Modern Adaptations

Contemporary couples balance tradition with practicality:

  • Professional fortune telling apps: 1,000-2,000 TWD ($30-65 USD)
  • Weekend preference consideration (70% choose Saturdays)
  • Seasonal weather factors influence final selection
  • Virtual consultations for overseas family members

Guo Da Ligwoh-dah-lee: The Betrothal Gift Exchange Ceremony

Guo da li is a formal betrothal ceremony where the groom’s family presents symbolic gifts to the bride’s family, typically occurring 1-3 months before the wedding and involving gifts worth 50,000-200,000 TWD ($1,600-6,500 USD).

Traditional Gift Requirements and Costs

  • Gold jewelry set: 80,000-150,000 TWD ($2,600-4,900 USD)
  • Wedding cakes (xi bingshee-bing): 10,000-30,000 TWD ($325-975 USD)
  • Red envelope (pin jinpin-jin): 60,000-360,000 TWD ($2,000-12,000 USD)
  • Traditional items: Tea, wine, fruits totaling 5,000-10,000 TWD ($160-325 USD)

The Guo Da Li Process

  1. Matchmaker coordination: Hao ming pohow-ming-poh arranges gift presentation date
  2. Gift preparation: 12 specific items representing fertility and prosperity
  3. Formal presentation: Groom’s family arrives with gifts in even numbers
  4. Acceptance ritual: Bride’s family examines and formally accepts gifts

Gift Categories and Symbolism

Essential six gifts (liu lileo-lee) include:

  • Dragon and phoenix candles: Eternal union
  • Bridal cakes: Announcing the marriage
  • Gold jewelry: Family wealth and status
  • Tea leaves: Respect for elders
  • Wine: Celebration and joy
  • Sticky rice: Family cohesion

Return gifts (hui lihway-lee) from bride’s family:

  • Portion of wedding cakes: 30-50% returned
  • Symbolic items: Lotus seeds for fertility
  • Red envelopes: 10-30% of received amount

Contemporary Practices

Modern guo da li ceremonies adapt to urban lifestyles:

  • Simplified 6-gift sets: 30,000-80,000 TWD ($975-2,600 USD)
  • Digital red envelopes via banking apps
  • Professional gift packaging services: 2,000-5,000 TWD ($65-160 USD)
  • Photography documentation replacing formal witnessing

Ding Hunding-hwun: The Formal Engagement Ceremony

Ding hun is the official engagement celebration hosted by the bride’s family, typically occurring 2-3 months before the wedding and involving 50-150 guests at a cost of 100,000-300,000 TWD ($3,250-9,750 USD).

Ceremony Structure and Timeline

  • Preparation phase: 2-3 hours for setup and decoration
  • Guest arrival: 30-minute reception period
  • Formal ceremony: 1-hour program including speeches
  • Banquet duration: 2-3 hours with 8-10 courses

Engagement Ceremony Elements

  1. Venue selection: Hotel banquet halls or restaurants
  2. Guest list compilation: Average 80-120 attendees
  3. Ring exchange ceremony: Western influence addition
  4. Elder blessing ritual: Tea serving to grandparents

Regional Engagement Variations

Taipei metropolitan customs feature elaborate hotel ceremonies with professional wedding planners, averaging 200,000-400,000 TWD ($6,500-13,000 USD) for 100+ guests.

Rural Taiwan traditions maintain home-based celebrations with community involvement, typically costing 50,000-150,000 TWD ($1,625-4,875 USD) for similar guest counts.

Hair Combing Ceremony: Transition to Adulthood

Shu toushoo-toh is a blessing ceremony performed the night before the wedding where an elder woman of good fortune combs the bride’s hair four times, typically lasting 30-45 minutes and costing 3,000-8,000 TWD ($100-260 USD) for the ceremonial items.

Ceremony Requirements

  • Good fortune woman criteria: Living parents, spouse, and children
  • Ceremonial tools: New comb, red thread, mirror
  • Blessing recitation: Four specific prosperity verses
  • Time: Usually 9-11 PM (auspicious evening hours)

The Four Combing Blessings

  1. First combing: “May you be blessed from beginning to end”
  2. Second combing: “May you have harmonious marriage until old age”
  3. Third combing: “May you have many children and grandchildren”
  4. Fourth combing: “May you have wealth and longevity”

Wedding Day Traditions

Chuangmenchwahng-men: The Door Games Challenge

Chuangmen is a playful tradition where bridesmaids create obstacles for the groom and his groomsmen, typically lasting 30-60 minutes and involving red envelope bribes totaling 6,000-18,000 TWD ($195-585 USD).

Game Categories and Costs

  • Knowledge tests: Questions about bride’s preferences
  • Red envelope negotiations: 600-1,200 TWD ($20-40 USD) per bridesmaid

Popular Door Game Challenges

  2. Memory test: Identify bride’s lipstick mark among others
  3. Endurance challenge: Hold difficult poses while answering questions
  4. Creativity test: Compose impromptu love song or poem

Modern Door Game Trends

  • Themed games matching couple’s interests

Jing Chajing-chah: The Traditional Tea Ceremony

Jing cha is a reverential ceremony where the couple serves tea to elders in order of seniority, typically lasting 1-2 hours and involving the exchange of gold jewelry and red envelopes worth 60,000-360,000 TWD ($2,000-12,000 USD) total.

Tea Ceremony Structure

  • Preparation: 30 minutes for tea brewing and setup
  • Service order: Parents, grandparents, aunts/uncles
  • Duration per elder: 3-5 minutes including blessings
  • Total participants: 10-30 family members

The Tea Service Protocol

  1. Tea preparation: Sweet tea using longan and red dates
  2. Kneeling position: Couple kneels on red cushions
  3. Two-handed service: Bride holds tray, groom assists
  4. Blessing exchange: Elders give advice and gifts

Gift Exchange During Tea Ceremony

From elders to couple:

  • Gold jewelry: 20,000-60,000 TWD ($650-2,000 USD) per elder couple
  • Red envelopes: 6,000-36,000 TWD ($200-1,200 USD) per person
  • Family heirlooms: Jade bracelets, gold necklaces

Regional tea ceremony variations:

  • Northern Taiwan: Formal kneeling throughout entire ceremony
  • Southern Taiwan: Modified standing positions acceptable
  • Eastern Taiwan: Indigenous influences with ceremonial wine addition

Xi Yanshee-yen: The Wedding Banquet Reception

Xishee yan is the elaborate wedding feast hosted by the groom’s family, typically featuring 10-12 courses for 200-500 guests at a cost of 600,000-2,000,000 TWD ($19,500-65,000 USD) for the entire event.

Banquet Cost Breakdown

  • Venue rental: 50,000-200,000 TWD ($1,625-6,500 USD)
  • Per table cost: 10,000-30,000 TWD ($325-975 USD) for 10 guests
  • Total tables: Average 20-30 tables
  • Additional services: MC, photography, decorations

Symbolic Banquet Dishes

  1. Whole roasted pig: Abundance and prosperity
  2. Steamed whole fish: Surplus year after year
  3. Lobster or prawns: Dragon symbol for good fortune
  4. Shark fin soup: Prestige (often replaced with alternatives)
  5. Abalone: Wealth and status
  6. Bird’s nest soup: Health and longevity
  7. Roasted duck: Fidelity and peace
  8. Sea cucumber: Flexibility in marriage
  9. Mushroom and vegetables: Continuing growth
  10. Lotus seed dessert: Fertility blessing
  11. Fresh fruit platter: Sweet future
  12. Wedding cake: Western influence addition

Modern Banquet Adaptations

Contemporary xi yan incorporates:

  • Vegetarian menu options: 8,000-15,000 TWD ($260-490 USD) per table
  • Wine pairing service: 5,000-10,000 TWD ($160-325 USD) per table
  • LED screens for photo montages
  • Live streaming for overseas relatives

Outfit Progression and Timing

Outfit Categories and Costs

Post-Wedding Traditions

San Chaosahn-chow: The Third Day Return Visit

San chao is the bride’s formal return visit to her parental home on the third day after the wedding, typically involving a 4-6 hour visit with gifts worth 10,000-30,000 TWD ($325-975 USD).

Return Visit Protocol

  • Timing: Morning arrival (before noon)
  • Gifts brought: Roasted pig, cakes, fruits
  • Participants: Newlywed couple only
  • Duration: Lunch through afternoon

San Chao Modern Practices

Contemporary couples adapt the tradition:

  • Weekend scheduling for working couples
  • Restaurant meals replacing home cooking
  • Combined with honeymoon departure
  • Video calls for overseas families

Wedding Gift Return Customs

Hui lihway-lee is the practice of the bride’s family returning a portion of the betrothal gifts, typically 30-50% of the original value, occurring within one month after the wedding.

Return Gift Categories

  • Mandatory returns: 30% of wedding cakes
  • Optional returns: Portion of gold jewelry value
  • Additional gifts: Baby clothes (fertility wishes)
  • Modern practice: Cash equivalent returns

How much does a typical Taiwanese wedding cost?

A typical Taiwanese wedding costs between 800,000-2,000,000 TWD ($26,000-65,000 USD) total, including all ceremonies and receptions. The breakdown includes: betrothal gifts (guo da ligwoh-dah-lee) averaging 100,000-200,000 TWD, engagement ceremony (ding hunding-hwun) at 100,000-300,000 TWD, wedding banquet (xi yanshee-yen) ranging from 600,000-1,500,000 TWD for 200-300 guests, and pre-wedding photography at 80,000-200,000 TWD. Red envelope gifts from guests typically recover 50-70% of banquet costs, with urban guests giving 3,600-6,000 TWD per person and rural guests giving 2,000-3,600 TWD. Couples often spend 6-12 months saving for their wedding, with many receiving family financial assistance for major expenses.

What is the significance of the tea ceremony (jing chajing-chah) in Taiwanese weddings?

The tea ceremony (jing cha) is a cornerstone tradition demonstrating respect for elders and formally uniting two families. During this 1-2 hour ceremony, the couple kneels on red cushions to serve sweet longan tea to parents, grandparents, and elder relatives in hierarchical order. Each elder offers marriage advice and presents gifts-typically gold jewelry worth 20,000-60,000 TWD or red envelopes containing 6,000-36,000 TWD. The ceremony’s Confucian roots emphasize filial piety, with the bride formally entering the groom’s family through this ritual. Modern adaptations may allow standing positions or shortened guest lists, but 95% of Taiwanese weddings retain this meaningful tradition. The total value of gifts received often ranges from 60,000-360,000 TWD, helping offset wedding expenses.

How long do Taiwanese wedding celebrations typically last?

Traditional Taiwanese wedding celebrations span 2-7 days with multiple events, though modern weddings often condense to 2-3 days. The timeline includes: auspicious date selection (6-12 months prior), betrothal ceremony (guo da li, 1-3 months before), engagement party (ding hun, 2-3 months before), hair combing ceremony (shu toushoo-toh, night before), wedding day ceremonies (12-15 hours), and the bride’s return visit (san chaosahn-chow, 3 days after). The main wedding day itself runs 12-15 hours, starting with morning preparations at 6 AM, door games (chuangmenchwahng-men) at 9 AM, tea ceremony at 11 AM, and evening banquet from 6-10 PM. Multi-day celebrations remain common in southern Taiwan (65% of weddings), while northern urban areas favor consolidated 1-2 day events.

What are door games (chuangmen) and why are they important?

Door games (chuangmen) are playful challenges lasting 30-60 minutes where bridesmaids test the groom’s determination before allowing him to collect the bride. These games include physical challenges (push-ups while reciting vows), knowledge tests (identifying the bride’s lipstick mark), and creative tasks (singing love songs in multiple languages). The groom and groomsmen must also offer red envelopes totaling 6,000-18,000 TWD as “bribes” to pass each challenge. This tradition, rooted in ancient customs of proving worthiness, creates memorable moments and photos while building excitement for the day. Modern couples often live-stream these games for distant relatives, and 90% of Taiwanese weddings include some form of door games, even in simplified ceremonies.

What foods are essential at a Taiwanese wedding banquet?

A traditional Taiwanese wedding banquet (xishee yan) features 10-12 symbolic courses costing 10,000-30,000 TWD per table. Essential dishes include: whole roasted pig (abundance), steamed whole fish (yearly surplus), lobster (dragon fortune), abalone (wealth), bird’s nest soup (health), roasted duck (fidelity), sea cucumber (flexibility), mushroom medley (growth), lotus seed dessert (fertility), and fresh fruit (sweetness). Each dish’s name and appearance carry auspicious meanings-fish (yuyew) sounds like surplus, while lotus seeds (lian zilyen-dzuh) sound like “continuous sons.” Modern banquets may substitute shark fin with premium mushroom soup and offer vegetarian alternatives. The meal progression follows strict traditions, with seafood preceding land animals and sweet soup concluding the feast.
