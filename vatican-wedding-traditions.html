Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a Vatican City wedding cost?

A Vatican City wedding typically costs between €15,000-€50,000 ($16,500-$55,000 USD), including ceremony fees, documentation, music, and reception expenses.

How long does it take to plan a Vatican wedding?

Planning requires 12-18 months, including Pre-Cana completion, document preparation, and obtaining the Nihil Obstat approval.

What is Pre-Cana and why is it required?

Pre-Cana is a mandatory Catholic marriage preparation course covering theology, relationship skills, and faith topics. It's required for Vatican wedding approval.

Are there dress code requirements for Vatican weddings?

Yes, brides must wear modest gowns with covered shoulders and appropriate length. Grooms wear formal morning dress or black tuxedos.

Can non-Catholics get married in Vatican City?

At least one partner must be Catholic. Mixed-faith marriages require special permissions and additional documentation.

What is the Sposi Novelli blessing?

It's a special papal blessing for newlyweds, typically given during Wednesday general audiences in St. Peter's Square.

How many guests can attend a Vatican wedding?

Vatican weddings typically accommodate 50-150 guests, depending on the specific chapel or basilica location chosen.

What documents are needed for a Vatican wedding?

Required documents include baptismal certificates, confirmation records, Pre-Cana certificate, and various church and civil affidavits.

Is music restricted at Vatican weddings?

Yes, only approved sacred music (musica sacra) is permitted, performed by authorized Vatican musicians.

Can we have a reception in Vatican City?

Receptions typically take place outside Vatican City at nearby Roman venues, as space within is limited to ceremonial functions.
