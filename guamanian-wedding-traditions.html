Pre-Wedding Traditions: Mamaisen Saina, Fandanggo, and Komplimentu

Mamaisen Sainamah-MY-sen SIGH-nahElder Consultation Ceremony

Mamaisen sainaSIGH-nah is a Chamorro proposal tradition that requires the groom’s family to formally visit the bride’s elders for marriage approval, typically occurring 6-12 months before the wedding and involving 10-20 family members. This 1-2 hour consultation costs $100-$500 USD for traditional gifts including pupulupoo-POO-loobetel nut and occurs at the bride’s family home. The ceremony reflects Guam’s matrilineal heritage where women historically controlled land ownership and family decisions.

The groom’s mother or senior female relative presents the pupulu while discussing family compatibility and marriage intentions. Modern couples in 2024 often conduct virtual mamaisen saina sessions for diaspora families, though 80% of rural Chamorro families maintain full traditional protocols. Fandanggo is a lively pre-wedding party held 1-2 days before the main ceremony, lasting 4-6 hours with 50-150 guests and costing $1,000-$5,000 USD. Derived from Spanish “fandango” but infused with Chamorro batsuBAHT-soo dances, this celebration occurs at family homes or community halls. Families perform traditional songs and dances while sharing red rice, kelaguenkeh-LAH-gwenmarinated meat, and lechonleh-CHONroast pig.

The celebration reinforces reciprocal relationships through chenchule’ (gift-giving) and allows elders to share marital wisdom through formal toasts.

Catholic Wedding Mass with BeluBEH-loo and ArrasAH-rahs

Belu is a sacred veil ceremony performed during Catholic mass where godparents drape a white veil over the couple, symbolizing unity under God’s protection and lasting 5-10 minutes with all wedding guests present. Arras is a coin exchange ritual where the groom presents 13 gold or silver coins to the bride, who returns them to demonstrate mutual trust and shared prosperity, costing $50-$100 USD for ceremonial coins. Mangnginge’ is a post-ceremony tradition where newlyweds demonstrate respect by sniffing the hands of all elders present, typically lasting 1 hour with 50-100 participants at the reception venue.
